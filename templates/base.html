Benefits of recycling

Waste Reduction

Landfills are ill-equipped to handle the amount of trash dumped each year in the United States. For this reason, overflow is sent to incinerators. Certain waste releases toxic chemicals into the air when burned, wreaking havoc on the environment. One of the benefits of recycling is that it significantly reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills and to incinerators, which ultimately translates into less pollution. Conservation of Natural Resources

Recycling also decreases our dependence on natural resources like certain minerals, water and wood, of which there is a finite supply. Based on our current need for raw materials, without recycling, the earth could not sustain the level of excavation required to support the economy. Wood, water, and metals can all be reduced and reused to the benefit of both the economy and humanity.

Reduction of Industrial Pollution

The collection of raw materials, like oil, requires heavy machinery. Most of these machines run on diesel fuel, which releases toxins into the air. With recycling, the need for raw materials is decreased, and with it, the pollution created by raw material excavation. Oil can be recycled countless times. If we chose not to recycle oil, we would need to consume it at a much higher rate in order to support the economy.

Reduction of Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Since recycling reduces the carbon footprint of industrialization via reduction and reuse of materials already in circulation, it is also effective in the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions created by manufacturers. Many manufacturers are finding that they can save money by reselling recycled product, and they choose not to run their factories at full power as a result. This results in lower fuel emissions.

Recycling Creates Jobs

Finally, recycling creates jobs in recycling plants and other manufacturing facilities that use recycled material. For this reason, recycling can help the economy as well as the environment. It is important to note that recycling facilities are growing in number and will need labor to fill important positions. Manufacturers who use recycled material will soon have the same need.

Energy Efficiency

One of the more subtle benefits of recycling is that it reduces energy consumption. It is more energy efficient to reduce and reuse material already in circulation than it is to produce the same material from scratch. Plastic bottles can be reduced and made into gloves. Newspaper can be recycled and reused in printing. Almost anything can be reduced and made into something else as long as its reduction is not toxic to the environment. it.
